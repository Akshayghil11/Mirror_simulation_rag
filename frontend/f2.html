Introduction to Reflection by Convex and Concave Mirrors

Welcome to our interactive physics simulation, designed to help you explore the fascinating world of light reflection using convex and concave mirrors.

Understanding Reflection in Curved Mirrors

Reflection is the phenomenon where light bounces off a surface, following the law of reflection: the angle of incidence is equal to the angle of reflection.

Concave Mirrors

  • A concave mirror (converging mirror) curves inward like the inside of a spoon.
  • These mirrors focus parallel incoming rays to a single focal point.
  • Depending on object position: Real/inverted images beyond F, Virtual/upright images between mirror and F.

Convex Mirrors

  • A convex mirror (diverging mirror) bulges outward.
  • Always produces Virtual, upright, and diminished images.

Mirror Formula

1/f = 1/v + 1/u

Learning Objectives

  • Understand reflection principles in concave and convex mirrors.
  • Observe how object distance affects image properties.
  • Learn to construct ray diagrams for image formation.
